"As colors fill the air, Holi reminds us to fill our lives with positivity, compassion, and harmony."

Introduction

Observed on the full moon day (Purnima) of the Hindu month of Phalguna, Holi marks the arrival of spring, the victory of good over evil, and the celebration of love, unity, and renewal. It carries deep historical, spiritual, and cultural significance rooted in Hindu mythology.

🔥 Mythological Stories

1. Prahlada and Holika

King Hiranyakashipu’s sister Holika sat in fire with Prahlada on her lap. Holika was burned, but Prahlada survived.

• Victory of good over evil • Power of devotion and faith • Destruction of arrogance and ego

2. Krishna and Radha

Lord Krishna applied color to Radha’s face to celebrate love beyond physical differences.

• Love beyond differences • Playfulness and romance • Origin of playing with colors
